1. INTRODUCTION

In 1932 Max Kleiber publish a paper titled "Body Size and Metabolism". He was interested in the relationship between an animal's size and the amount of energy it uses. For mammals, the rate at which an animal uses energy is known as the Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR). To determine the size of the animals Kleiber focused on the animal's mass. It was known that smaller animals have lower BMRs while larger animals have higher BMRs, but the relationship between size and BMR is not linear. The relationship took the form of a power law.
