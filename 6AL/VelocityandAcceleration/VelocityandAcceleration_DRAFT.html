PART 1: INTRODUCTION

For 1D motion of a body, we are typically concerned with three variables which characterize the trajectory:

  1. The position, x(t)x(t)x(t), which tells you where in space the body is located at a given time
  2. The velocity, v(t)v(t)v(t) which tells you the speed and direction the object is travelling at a given time, and hence the change in the position
  3. The acceleration, a(t)a(t)a(t), which accounts for the forces on the body through F=maF=maF=ma, and tells you the change in the velocity.

These three variables are interrelated, as each quantity tells you about the change over time of the previous.

  2. Similarly, the acceleration of an object at a certain point is the slope of the velocity-time graph at that point.
